## Building Technology That Improves Lives

Some engineers build products. Others build communities. Dr. Felix Tettey-Engmann has dedicated his career to doing both.

Driven by a belief that innovation should solve meaningful human problems, Felix has built a career that spans advanced semiconductor engineering, scientific research, entrepreneurship, education, and community leadership. His work reflects a simple philosophy: technology is most powerful when it creates opportunities for people.

Today, Felix serves as a Module Development Engineer at Intel Corporation, where he contributes to the development of next-generation semiconductor manufacturing technologies. Working with advanced EUV and DUV lithography systems, he helps optimize reticle processes, improve defect detection, analyze yield-limiting issues, and support the successful production of cutting-edge semiconductor devices. His work plays a role in the technologies that power modern computing, artificial intelligence, autonomous systems, and the digital infrastructure of tomorrow.

Before entering the semiconductor industry, Felix established himself as a respected researcher in biomaterials, nanotechnology, additive manufacturing, and tissue engineering. During his graduate studies at North Carolina Agricultural and Technical State University, he designed advanced biomaterials, developed 3D bioprinted tissue constructs, engineered electrospun nanofibrous scaffolds, and conducted multidisciplinary research that resulted in more than 20 peer-reviewed publications and over 550 citations. His research has earned national recognition and multiple awards for innovation and scientific excellence.

Yet Felix's impact extends well beyond laboratories and manufacturing facilities.

Throughout his journey, he has remained deeply committed to empowering students and creating opportunities for others. As Co-Founder and Chief Operating Officer of GRADCAB, he helped build a platform dedicated to making the transition into graduate school easier for international students by connecting newcomers with trusted student mentors, providing airport pickup services, and fostering a welcoming academic community.

He also serves as Head of Services at Inested LLC, where he leads initiatives connecting students with professors, researchers, and industry professionals. Through mentorship programs, publication collaborations, interview preparation, and career development services, he works to bridge the gap between education and professional success.

Felix's commitment to service has also been demonstrated through years of volunteer work. From mentoring students and teaching mathematics in underserved communities to judging regional science and engineering competitions, he believes that investing in young people is one of the most meaningful ways to create lasting impact.

His entrepreneurial mindset is matched by his passion for leadership. Whether coordinating multidisciplinary engineering teams, managing laboratory operations, building technology platforms, or leading community initiatives, Felix approaches every challenge with curiosity, integrity, and a focus on delivering measurable results.

His diverse background—from biomedical engineering and advanced manufacturing to semiconductor process development and operations leadership—allows him to connect ideas across disciplines. This ability to bridge research, engineering, business, and community engagement enables him to solve complex problems while maintaining a strong focus on the people those solutions ultimately serve.

Felix believes that engineering is more than designing systems or optimizing processes. It is about improving lives, expanding opportunities, and creating technologies that make society stronger. Every project he undertakes is guided by excellence, collaboration, continuous learning, and service to others.

As technology continues to evolve, his mission remains constant: to advance innovation that not only pushes the boundaries of science and engineering but also empowers individuals, strengthens communities, and leaves a lasting positive impact on the world.
