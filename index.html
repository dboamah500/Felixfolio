The Story

Innovation is most powerful when it creates opportunities for people

Felix serves as a Module Development Engineer at Intel Corporation, contributing to next-generation semiconductor manufacturing. Working with advanced EUV and DUV lithography systems, he optimizes reticle processes, improves defect detection, and analyzes yield-limiting issues behind the devices that power modern computing, artificial intelligence, and the digital infrastructure of tomorrow.

Before entering the semiconductor industry, he established himself as a respected researcher in biomaterials, nanotechnology, additive manufacturing, and tissue engineering at North Carolina A&T State University — designing advanced biomaterials, developing 3D bioprinted tissue constructs, and engineering electrospun nanofibrous scaffolds. That work produced more than 20 peer-reviewed publications, over 550 citations, and multiple awards for innovation and scientific excellence.

Beyond the lab and the fab, Felix co-founded GRADCAB to ease the transition into graduate school for international students, and leads services at Inested LLC connecting students with professors, researchers, and industry professionals. From mentoring students and teaching mathematics in underserved communities to judging regional science and engineering competitions, he believes investing in young people is one of the most meaningful ways to create lasting impact.
