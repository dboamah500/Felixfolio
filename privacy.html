Privacy Policy

This site is a personal portfolio. It has no accounts, no tracking pixels, and no database — this page explains the little that does happen with your data.

01

Introduction

This website (the personal portfolio of Dr. Felix Tettey-Engmann) is a static site: it exists to present Felix’s work, research, and contact details. It does not require registration, does not set advertising cookies, and does not sell or trade personal information.

By using the site you agree to the practices described here. If anything is unclear, contact details are at the bottom of this page.

02

Information We Collect

The site itself stores nothing about you. The only information that can reach Felix is what you choose to send.

Personal Information

Provided only if you choose to reach out:

  • Your name and email address, if you use the contact form (it opens a draft in your own email app — nothing is stored on this site)
  • Anything you write in an email, LinkedIn message, or WhatsApp chat
  • Your phone number, if you call or text

Automated Data Collection

Standard technical requests made by your browser:

  • The web host serving these pages may keep routine access logs (IP address, page requested, time)
  • Fonts load from Google Fonts, so your browser requests files from Google’s servers
  • No analytics scripts, advertising trackers, or fingerprinting run on this site
03

How We Use Your Information

Anything you send is used only to respond to you. Specifically:

Replying to your message or inquiry
Discussing collaboration, speaking, or mentorship
Scheduling calls or meetings you request
Sharing research or career materials you ask for
Keeping a record of our correspondence
Nothing else — no marketing lists, no resale
04

Information Sharing and Disclosure

Your information is not sold, rented, or shared with third parties, with two narrow exceptions:

With Your Consent

If you ask Felix to pass your details to someone (for example, an introduction or a reference), they are shared only as you requested.

Legal Requirements

Correspondence may be disclosed if the law genuinely requires it, for example:

  • A valid court order or subpoena
  • A lawful request by a government authority
  • Protecting against fraud or abuse directed at this site
  • Establishing or defending legal claims
05

Data Security

Because the site stores nothing, the main safeguards apply to how your messages are handled once received:

Encryption

The site is served over HTTPS, so pages and the resume download travel encrypted

Access Control

Email accounts used for correspondence are protected with strong authentication

Data Protection

Messages are kept only as long as a genuine correspondence needs them

06

Your Rights and Choices

Since the only data is what you’ve sent, exercising your rights is simple — just ask:

Access Your Data

Ask what correspondence of yours is on file

Correct Information

Ask for anything inaccurate to be corrected

Delete Your Data

Ask for your messages and details to be deleted

Object to Processing

Ask that your information not be used at all

07

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes — for example, if analytics are ever added — this page will be updated and the “Last Updated” date revised.

Substantive changes will be noted plainly at the top of this page.
